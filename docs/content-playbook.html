Section 02

Voice & Tone Rules

Sound like a sharp friend who knows what they're doing. Not a corporate brochure.

Rule 01
Direct, not aggressive
Say what you mean in as few words as possible. Don't soften with "just" or "maybe." But don't be a jerk about it either.
Rule 02
Honest, not arrogant
Challenge bad thinking. Say what others won't. But back it up with proof, not ego. "We'd rather be honest" is the energy.
Rule 03
Specific, not vague
Numbers beat adjectives. "53% of visitors leave after 3 seconds" beats "many visitors leave quickly." Use metrics. Name tools. Be concrete.
Rule 04
Problem-first, always
Never start with "We build..." Start with "You're losing..." The audience doesn't care about you. They care about their problem.

Words We Never Use

Banned Words
leverage · synergy · digital transformation · crafting excellence world-class · elevate · revolutionary · game-changer holistic · cutting-edge · solution-oriented · innovative best-in-class · disrupting · empowering · ecosystem

Copy Rules

Do This
"Your website should be your best salesperson." "We hand-code everything. No templates." "Our last 5 projects averaged 96 PageSpeed." "53% of visitors leave after 3 seconds."
Not This
"We build premium bespoke websites." "Our holistic solutions leverage cutting-edge tech." "We're the best agency for your needs." "Many visitors leave quickly."

Formatting Rules

  • No em dashes. Use periods or commas. "We build websites. Custom coded." Not "We build websites — custom coded."
  • One CTA per section. Don't give 5 choices. One clear next step: "See what we built."
  • Short paragraphs. Max 3 sentences. If it looks dense, break it up.
  • Show, don't claim. Proof beats assertions. Screenshots beat promises. Data beats opinions.
Section 03

The PAS Framework

Every piece of content follows this structure. No exceptions.

Problem → Agitate → Solve

The copywriting engine behind every post, video, caption, and ad.

Step 1
Problem
Name the pain. Be specific. Make them feel seen. "Your website loads in 6 seconds. Half your visitors are already gone."
Step 2
Agitate
Turn up the heat. Show what happens if they do nothing. "Every day that site is live, you're paying for traffic that bounces."
Step 3
Solve
Present the fix. Be concrete. "We rebuilt it in 3 weeks. Load time: 1.4s. Bounce rate dropped 40%."

Why PAS Works

People don't buy solutions. They buy relief from pain. PAS puts the pain first, makes it feel urgent, then positions you as the obvious fix. It works because it mirrors how people already think about their problems.

PAS In Action — Short-Form Example

Instagram / TikTok Caption
P: Your website takes 6 seconds to load.
A: That means 53% of your visitors never see your homepage. They leave before the logo loads. You're paying for traffic that bounces.
S: We rebuilt a client's site last month. Load time went from 6.2s to 1.4s. Enquiries doubled in two weeks. Link in bio for a free audit.

PAS In Action — Video Script (15-30s)

Short-Form Video
[0-5s] Problem: Bold text on screen. Hook the viewer. "Your website is losing you money."
[5-15s] Agitate: Show the data. Red metrics. Slow load. Bad mobile view. "6.2 second load time. 53% bounce rate. You're invisible on Google."
[15-25s] Solve: Flip to green. Fast load. Clean design. Real metrics. "We rebuilt it. 1.4s load. 40% more enquiries."
[25-30s] CTA: Logo + one line. "Free site audit. Link in bio."

Key Principle: One Concept Per Post

If you can't explain the idea in one sentence, you're trying to say too much for a single post. Split it into two.
Adapted from Jack Butcher's "Visualize Value" philosophy

Every post should communicate one clear idea. Not three insights packaged together. One sharp thought, visualized clearly, with a single CTA.

Section 04

Content Pillars

Every post fits into one of these four categories. Rotate through them.

🔴
1. Problem Exposure
Show businesses what's broken about their website. Use data, audits, and real examples to make the pain visible.
Examples:
"Your site takes 6s to load. Here's what that costs you."
"I audited 10 local business websites. 8 failed basic SEO."
"This is what your website looks like on mobile. Yikes."
🟡
2. Contrarian Takes
Challenge conventional thinking. Say what agencies won't. Cross out the old way, state the real way.
Examples:
"Your website doesn't need a redesign. It needs a rebuild."
"Stop asking 'does it look good?' Start asking 'does it convert?'"
"Templates are why your site looks like everyone else's."
🟢
3. Process & Education
Teach how good websites work. Break down conversion thinking, design principles, and the TRAIN system.
Examples:
"The 5 things every homepage needs (most have 0)."
"Why your hero section should talk about THEM, not you."
"Here's the exact process we use: Discover → Design → Develop → Deliver."
⚪
4. Proof & Results
Show the work. Before/after metrics. Client conversations. PageSpeed scores. Real results, not claims.
Examples:
"Client site went from 6.2s → 1.4s load time. Here's the rebuild."
"PageSpeed: 23 → 96. Same content. Different build."
"This client got 3x more enquiries after we rebuilt their site."

Pillar Rotation

Don't post the same type three times in a row. Rotate through pillars to keep the feed balanced. A good rhythm:

Day Pillar Why
Mon Problem Exposure Start the week by making the pain visible
Wed Contrarian Take Mid-week engagement driver (hot takes spark comments)
Fri Proof & Results End the week with credibility. People research on weekends.
Sun (optional) Education Position as expert. Build trust for the Monday sell.

Color Rules

  • Max 2–3 colors per post. Dark background + white text + one accent. That's it.
  • Red = problems. Slow load times, broken things, what's wrong. Always #ef4444.
  • Green = solutions. Fast loads, conversions, results. Always #10b981.
  • Never use color for decoration. Every color communicates something.

Typography

Primary Font
Inter
All body text, captions, and UI elements. Weights: 400 (body), 600 (emphasis), 700 (subheads), 900 (headlines).
Logo Font
Classicismo
Used exclusively for the CrftdWeb logo mark. Never for body text or headlines.

Type Rules

  • Headlines: Inter 900, tight tracking. Big, bold, minimal. Let the weight do the work.
  • Max 2 font families. Never introduce a third typeface. Ever.
  • Uppercase labels only. Small caps with wide letter-spacing (0.2em) for category labels, section names, and tags.
  • Body text stays at 400 weight. Don't bold entire paragraphs. Bold individual words for emphasis.

Green accents, flipped metrics, clean design. Show the transformation. Concrete results.

→

Section 12

Value Positioning

Don't sell what you do. Sell the value of what you do for them. Frame everything around the client's outcome.

The Value Ladder

Not all work is valued equally. Position yourself in the "Can't Do" zone. That's where premium pricing lives.

Low Value
"Don't Do"
Easy, non-specific tasks anyone can do. Squarespace templates. Fiverr gigs. Commodity work. The market is flooded and price is the only differentiator.
Medium Value
"Won't Do"
Specific tasks that require commitment and consistency. Running a proper web design service. Most agencies sit here.
High Value
"Can't Do"
Ultra-specific, high-expertise work. Hand-coded conversion systems. TRAIN methodology. Proprietary process. This is where CrftdWeb lives.
Content Angle
Show the difference
Your content should constantly illustrate the gap between template sites and custom builds. Make the value visible.

Four Types of Value

Every piece of content should deliver at least one of these. The best content delivers two or three.

TypeWhat It MeansContent Example
💰 Monetary Help them make money "This website change doubled our client's enquiries in 2 weeks."
🔧 Functional Help them do something better "5 things your homepage needs (most have 0)."
🧠 Psychological Help them feel better "Your website isn't your fault. But fixing it is your responsibility."
🤝 Social Help them fit in / stand out "Your competitors' websites look like this. Yours looks like this."

The 10X Pricing Rule

At this price point, can your customer get a 10X return on investment?

Use this in your content to justify pricing. Don't defend your prices. Frame the ROI.

Defensive
"Our prices reflect the quality of work." "We charge more because we're better."
ROI-Framed
"A £2,497 site that brings in £25k of new business. That's a 10X return." "What's one new client worth to you? Now multiply by 12 months."

The Value Gap

Content that sells identifies the gap between where the client is and where they want to be. Your job is to make that gap obvious, then show you can close it.

1
Name the pain point

"Your website loads in 6 seconds. You're losing 53% of visitors before they see your offer."

2
Quantify the cost

"If 10 people visit daily on average, that's 5 lost leads. 150 per month. What's each lead worth?"

3
Show the fix is simple

"We rebuilt a client's site in 3 weeks. Load time: 1.4s. Bounce rate dropped 40%. Enquiries doubled."

4
Make it impossible to say no

"Free audit. No commitment. We'll show you exactly what's costing you money."

Section 13

The 80/20 Rule &
Organic Strategies

80% of your content gives value. 20% asks for it. Get this ratio wrong and you become noise.

The 80/20 Reciprocity Rule

80% — Give Value
Build equity in the market
Teach. Share. Help. Create. Show your process. Give away insights. Build trust before you ever ask for anything.
20% — Ask
Collect dividends
Sell. Promote. Launch. "Book a call." "Link in bio."

If 5 posts go out this week, 4 should be pure value (education, audits, insights, behind-the-scenes). Only 1 should directly sell.

10 Organic Marketing Strategies (£0 Ad Spend)

#StrategyHow to Apply It
01 Create Urgency "Taking 2 more projects this month." Limited availability, time-bound offers.
02 Answer Questions in Public Turn FAQs into posts. "How much does a website cost?" becomes content.
03 Endorsements Let clients do the marketing. Share testimonials, screenshots, results.
04 The Reverse Pitch "If you're happy with your current site's performance, this isn't for you."
05 Problem / Solution Specific problem → you as the answer. This IS the PAS framework.
06 Permissionless Proof Audit random websites publicly. Redesign them without being asked. Show what you'd fix.
07 Case Studies Before/after on social. PageSpeed scores. Enquiry numbers. Real results.
08 Transparency Share revenue. Share failures. Share process. Openness builds trust.

What "Good" Looks Like

Red Flag
High reach, zero DMs
Your content is entertaining but not converting. You're getting views but no action. Fix the CTA. Make the next step obvious.
Green Flag
Low reach, high saves
Small audience but deeply engaged. This is better than viral with no follow-through. Keep going. The audience will grow.
Red Flag
Followers up, enquiries flat
You're attracting the wrong audience or your 80/20 ratio is off. Check: are you actually asking for the sale 20% of the time?
Green Flag
DMs saying "I need this"
Your PAS is working. They feel the pain, they see the solution, they're reaching out. This is the goal. Close them.

Section 17

Pre-Publish Checklist

Run through this before every piece of content goes live.

Content Quality

  • Does it follow PAS? (Problem → Agitate → Solve)
  • Is the hook strong enough to stop someone scrolling?
  • One concept only? If you're saying two things, split into two posts.
  • Problem-first framing? (not "we build..." but "you're losing...")
  • Specific, not vague? (numbers, metrics, examples — not adjectives)
  • No banned words? (leverage, synergy, game-changer, world-class, etc.)
  • One CTA only? One clear next step, not three options.
  • Would you stop scrolling for this? Be honest.

Visual Quality

  • On-brand colors? (#0a0a0a background, white text, red/green accents)
  • Inter font only? (unless logo placement)
  • Max 2–3 colors in the design?
  • CW logo present and properly placed?
  • Safe zones respected? (no content in top 150px or bottom 480px for video)
  • Text is readable at mobile size? (not too small, enough contrast)
  • Negative space applied? (not cramped — let it breathe)

Platform Requirements

  • Correct dimensions? (1080×1920 video, 1080×1080 feed, 1080×1350 carousel)
  • Caption written with hook-first structure?
  • Hashtags included? (5–10, mixed size and niche)
  • CTA matches the content? (don't just default to "link in bio")
  • Cross-posted to all relevant platforms?
  • No TikTok watermark on Instagram version?

Value & Strategy

  • Does this deliver value? (monetary, functional, psychological, or social)
  • Is this an 80% post (give value) or a 20% post (ask for value)?
  • Does the CTA match the 80/20 ratio for the week?
  • Could this become a case study, testimonial, or proof piece later?

Final Gut Check

  • Does this sound like CrftdWeb? (direct, honest, sharp — not corporate)
  • Would a business owner feel this in their gut?
  • Is this something worth saving or sharing?
  • Are you proud of it?
