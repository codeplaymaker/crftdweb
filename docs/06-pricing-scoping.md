# Pricing & Scoping Framework

> For your eyes only. Not shared with clients. Use this to quote consistently and fast.
> All prices in GBP (£).

---

## Tier 1: Landing Page

**What it is:** Single page. One goal. One CTA.
**Best for:** Product launches, lead magnets, event signups, waitlists.

**Includes:**
- 1 page (hero, features/benefits, social proof, CTA)
- Mobile-responsive
- Contact form or email capture
- Basic SEO (title, meta, OG tags)
- Vercel deployment
- 1 round of revisions

**Does NOT include:**
- Copywriting (client provides or pays extra)
- Brand identity/logo design
- Photography or illustration
- Ongoing maintenance

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks
**Price range:** £1,200 – £2,400

---

## Tier 2: Business Website

**What it is:** Full website. 5–7 pages. Conversion-focused.
**Best for:** Service businesses, startups, agencies, consultants.

**Includes:**
- 5–7 pages (Home, Services, Work/Portfolio, About, Contact + 1–2 custom)
- TRAIN design system applied
- Case study layout (Problem → Process → Result)
- Process flow section
- Contact form with email integration (Resend)
- Full SEO setup (meta, OG, sitemap, robots.txt)
- Google Analytics integration
- Mobile-first responsive
- 90+ PageSpeed
- Vercel deployment
- 2 rounds of revisions
- 2 weeks post-launch support

**Does NOT include:**
- Copywriting (guidance provided, client writes or pays extra)
- Brand identity/logo design (unless added)
- Photography or stock images
- User authentication
- Database/backend functionality
- Monthly maintenance (available as add-on)

**Timeline:** 3–5 weeks
**Price range:** £3,200 – £6,400

---

## Tier 3: Web Application

**What it is:** Custom web app with auth, database, dashboard.
**Best for:** SaaS products, client portals, internal tools, platforms.

**Includes:**
- Everything in Tier 2, plus:
- User authentication (Firebase Auth — signup, signin, password reset)
- Dashboard with custom UI
- Database integration (Firestore or Supabase)
- API integrations as needed
- Role-based access (if needed)
- Admin panel (if needed)
- 3 rounds of revisions
- 4 weeks post-launch support

**Does NOT include:**
- Ongoing feature development (available as retainer)
- Mobile app (React Native available as separate project)
- Payment processing (available as add-on)

**Timeline:** 6–10 weeks
**Price range:** £6,400 – £16,000+

## Scoping Questions (Ask Before Quoting)

1. **How many pages?** (determines tier)
2. **Do they need auth/database?** (Tier 2 vs Tier 3)
3. **Do they have copy?** (add copywriting or not)
4. **Do they have a brand?** (add brand identity or not)
5. **Do they have images/photos?** (need to source or not)
6. **What's their timeline?** (rush = premium)
7. **Do they need ongoing support?** (add maintenance retainer)
8. **What's their budget?** (qualify before investing time)

## Quoting Rules

1. **Never quote before the intake is filled out.** If they won't answer questions, they won't pay on time.
2. **50% upfront, 50% on launch.** No exceptions for new clients.
3. **Scope in writing.** If it's not in the scope doc, it's not included.
4. **Revisions are counted.** 1–3 rounds is fair. "Unlimited revisions" destroys profit.

## Quoting Rules

1. **Never quote before the intake is filled out.** If they won't answer questions, they won't pay on time.
2. **50% upfront, 50% on launch.** No exceptions for new clients.
3. **Scope in writing.** If it's not in the scope doc, it's not included.
4. **Revisions are counted.** "Unlimited revisions" destroys profit. 1-3 rounds is fair.
5. **Rush fee = 25-50% premium.** If they need it in half the time, charge accordingly.
6. **Say no to bad fits.** If the budget is $500 for a full site, refer them to a template.
7. **Site audit as a gateway.** Offer a paid audit first. If they like it, they hire for the rebuild.

## Red Flags (Walk Away)

- "Can you just do it for exposure?"
- "My nephew could do this but he's busy"
- "We need it tomorrow"
- "We'll pay when we start making money"
- "Can you match this Fiverr quote?"
- No clear business model or revenue
- Can't articulate who their customer is
- Want to "approve every pixel" (micromanagement)

## Green Flags (Great Client)

- Knows their customer well
- Has existing revenue or funding
- Respects your process
- Has real testimonials or proof to share
- Understands websites are investments, not expenses
- Responds to communications promptly
- Budget aligned with scope
