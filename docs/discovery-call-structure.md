# Discovery Call Structure
**CrftdWeb — Internal Use Only**

---

## Overview

**Goal:** Understand the prospect's situation, establish credibility, and close to a proposal or payment.  
**Length:** 20–30 minutes  
**Platform:** Google Meet — `meet.google.com/sht-kzhd-yxg`  
**Mindset:** Diagnose before you prescribe. Let them talk. The close is earned in the questioning phase.

---

## Before the Call (5 min)

- Check the booking — know their business name, what they said in the form
- Skim their current website if they have one
- Know which package fits them based on what they said
- Have the pricing ranges ready mentally:
  - Landing page: £1,200–£2,400
  - Business site: £3,200–£6,400
  - Web app / custom: £6,400+

---

## The Call Structure

### 1. Open (2 min)

**Purpose:** Make them comfortable. You're in control, but they should feel relaxed.

> "Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on — really appreciate it. Quick bit of housekeeping: I've set aside about 25 minutes for us today. What I want to do is understand where you're at with your website or online presence, then share what we think makes sense, and if it's a fit we can talk about next steps. Sound good?"

- Confirm they can hear/see you fine
- Keep it light and conversational

---

### 2. Diagnose the Problem (7 min)

**Purpose:** Get them talking. Understand the real pain. Do NOT pitch yet.

**Key questions (pick 3–5, don't rush through all of them):**

- "Tell me a bit about your business — what do you do and who do you sell to?"
- "What's your current website situation — do you have one, and if so what do you think of it?"
- "When a potential customer searches for what you offer, what happens? Are they finding you?"
- "How are most of your customers finding you right now?"
- "What's made you look into this now — has something changed?"
- "What would a successful website actually look like for you — more enquiries, more credibility, online sales?"
- "Is there a timeline you're working toward?"

**Listen for:**
- Pain: "my site looks outdated", "I'm not getting leads", "I've got nothing to show people"
- Urgency: launching something, new business, losing to competitors
- Goal: what winning looks like for them

**Don't:**
- Talk about yourself yet
- List features
- Quote a price

---

### 3. Reflect Back (2 min)

**Purpose:** Prove you listened. This builds trust faster than anything else.

> "Okay, so if I've understood correctly — you've got [situation], the main problem is [pain], and ideally you want to [goal] within [timeframe]. Did I get that right?"

Let them correct you. That's fine — it means they're engaged.

---

### 4. Present the Solution (4 min)

**Purpose:** Show them what you'd build and why it fits their specific situation.

> "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend..."

- Name the package (landing page / business site / web app)
- Explain 2–3 specific things you'd include and why they matter for their situation
- Mention the timeline
- Reference a work example if relevant

**Keep it specific to them.** Don't list everything CrftdWeb can do — just what they need.

> "What I'd build for you is [description]. The reason I'm suggesting this is [because of what they said]. Most projects like this take [X] weeks."

---

### 5. Handle Objections (5 min)

**The four objections and how to handle them:**

---

**"How much does it cost?"**

> "Sure — for what I just described, you're looking at somewhere between [£X] and [£Y]. I'll put together a full proposal with the exact number after this call. Payment is 50% upfront to kick off, 50% when we're ready to launch."

Don't apologise for the price. State it plainly.

---

**"That's more than I expected / seems expensive"**

> "I get that — it's a real investment. What I'd say is: what's the cost of not having this? If you're missing [2/3/5] clients a month because people can't find you or don't trust what they see, the site pays for itself pretty quickly. That said, we can look at the scope if you want to start smaller."

---

**"I need to think about it / talk to [partner/accountant]"**

> "Completely understand. What would be most useful — should I send the proposal over so you've got something concrete to look at and share? That way you've got all the detail in writing."

Then send the proposal. A "think about it" with a proposal in their inbox is a warm lead. Without it, it goes cold.

---

**"I already have someone / I'm talking to other agencies"**

> "That makes total sense — you should be comparing options. The one thing I'd say is what separates us is [speed / the quality of the code / having no middlemen / you deal directly with the person building it]. But I'll let the proposal speak for itself — see what you think."

---

### 6. Close (2 min)

**Purpose:** Lock in the next concrete action before hanging up.

**Best case — they're ready:**

> "Great. I'll put together the proposal and send it over today. Once you've had a look, if you want to move forward the process is: you sign the agreement, pay the deposit, and we're off. The whole thing takes about 5 minutes from your side."

**If they're not sure:**

> "No problem at all. I'll get the proposal to you today — it'll have everything we talked about, the price, the timeline, what's included. Just have a read and let me know. If you've got questions after reading it, reply to the email and we'll go from there."

**Always end with a concrete next step:**
- "I'll send the proposal today" — AND send it same day
- "When would be a good time to follow up?" — book it in now if possible

## Quick Reference: Pricing

| Package | Range | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page | £1,200 – £2,400 | 1–2 weeks |
| Business website | £3,200 – £6,400 | 2–4 weeks |
| Web application | £6,400+ | 4–10 weeks |

Payment: 50% deposit / 50% on launch, bank transfer  
Revisions: stated in scope (usually 2 rounds included)

---

## Quick Reference: Objection Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "How much?" | State the range plainly, explain 50/50 payment |
| "Too expensive" | ROI framing + offer to adjust scope if needed |
| "Need to think" | Offer to send proposal so they have something concrete |
| "Talking to others" | Acknowledge it, state your differentiator, let proposal do the work |
| "No time now" | "What's a better week for you?" — rebook, don't lose the lead |
