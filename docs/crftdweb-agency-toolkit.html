The TRAIN Design System

Every design decision follows five principles:

T

Typography

Max 2 font families. Weight, size, and spacing communicate before words do.

R

Restraint

Max 2-3 colors. Limited palette forces better ideas. Less noise, more signal.

A

Alignment

Invisible relationships create trust. Grids and geometry. Precision = trust.

I

Image Treatment

Consistent processing creates instant recognition. Same filter, same crop, same feel.

N

Negative Space

Space isn't there to fill. It's there to frame. Let the idea breathe.

Copy Principles

  1. Problem-first. Every headline answers "what's in it for the visitor."
  2. No fluff. Cut "leverage," "synergy," "digital transformation."
  3. No em dashes. Use periods or commas.
  4. No industry-boxing. State the problem, not the niche.
  5. Trust at every scroll point. Social proof, process visibility, risk reversal.
  6. Show, don't claim. "Here's what we built" beats "we're the best."
  7. One CTA per section. Don't give visitors 5 choices.
  8. Conversational, not corporate. Write for a smart friend.

Component Patterns

  • Hero: Problem headline + subtext + 2 CTAs + process mini-flow
  • Features: Outcomes, not capabilities
  • Case Studies: Problem → Process → Result cards with tech tags
  • Process Flow: Numbered steps (Discover → Design → Develop → Deliver)
  • TRAIN Grid: 5 cards showing the design system
  • Trust CTA: Urgency headline + "Free consultation · No commitment"
  • Contact Form: Name, email, message. No subject field.

Anti-Patterns

  • No template language ("elevate your brand," "world-class solutions")
  • No fake metrics or testimonials
  • No empty sub-pages
  • No features without outcomes
  • No "about us" before "about your problem"
  • No more than 2 font weights per page
  • No decorative elements that don't serve comprehension
  • No walls of text without visual breaks

Post-Launch Checklist

Run through before every deployment. Also use as an audit tool on existing sites.

1. Playbook Alignment

  • Every page headline is problem-first (about the visitor, not about you)
  • No template language ("digital transformation," "crafting excellence")
  • No fake metrics or testimonials
  • Trust signals present (proof, process visibility, risk reversal)
  • One clear CTA per section
  • No empty pages
  • Copy is conversational, not corporate

2. TRAIN Design Check

  • T: Max 2 font families, clear hierarchy, readable at all sizes
  • R: Max 2-3 colors used consistently
  • A: Grid consistency, nothing misaligned
  • I: All images processed consistently
  • N: Generous padding, content can breathe

3. Mobile QA

  • Tested at 375px (small phones)
  • Tested at 768px (tablets)
  • Tested at 1440px (desktop)
  • No horizontal scrolling
  • Text readable without zooming
  • Buttons tappable (44px min hit area)
  • Images don't overflow
  • Nav works on mobile

4. Performance

  • PageSpeed 90+ mobile
  • PageSpeed 90+ desktop
  • Sub-2s load time
  • Images optimized (WebP/AVIF, lazy loading)
  • No unused CSS/JS bundles
  • Fonts preloaded

5. SEO

  • Unique title on every page
  • Unique meta description on every page
  • Open Graph tags present
  • Twitter card meta present
  • Semantic HTML (h1 → h2 → h3)
  • Alt text on all images
  • Sitemap generated
  • robots.txt present

6. Functionality

  • All forms tested with real submissions
  • Validation works
  • Success states show
  • All internal links work
  • External links open in new tabs
  • Error pages styled (404, 500)
  • Loading states present

7. Accessibility

  • Keyboard navigation works
  • Focus indicators visible
  • Color contrast meets WCAG AA (4.5:1)
  • Screen reader friendly

8. Analytics & Tracking

  • Google Analytics installed
  • Form submissions tracked as conversions
  • Key pages have event tracking
  • No tracking scripts blocking render

9. Security & Hosting

  • HTTPS enabled
  • Env variables secured
  • No API keys in source
  • Domain configured
  • Favicon present

10. Final Visual Pass

  • Screenshot every page on mobile and desktop
  • Compare against TRAIN principles
  • Read all copy out loud
  • Have someone browse for 30 seconds and tell you what the site does

  1. Fill out Client Intake
  2. Update metadata in layout.tsx
  3. Swap hero copy with client's problem headline
  4. Update case studies in Work.tsx
  5. Customize services/process steps

Pricing & Scoping Framework

For your eyes only. Not shared with clients.

Service Tiers

Tier 1: Landing Page $1,500 – $3,000

Single page. One goal. One CTA. For product launches, lead magnets, waitlists.

Includes: 1 page, mobile-responsive, contact form, basic SEO, Vercel deploy, 1 revision round.

Excludes: Copywriting, brand identity, photography, ongoing maintenance.

Timeline: 1–2 weeks

Tier 2: Business Website $4,000 – $8,000

Full website. 5-7 pages. Conversion-focused. For service businesses, startups, agencies.

Includes: 5-7 pages, TRAIN applied, case studies, process flow, contact form, full SEO, GA4, mobile-first, 90+ PageSpeed, 2 revision rounds, 2 weeks post-launch support.

Excludes: Copywriting, brand identity, photography, auth/database, monthly maintenance.

Timeline: 3–5 weeks

Tier 3: Web Application $10,000 – $25,000+

Custom web app with auth, database, dashboard. For SaaS, client portals, platforms.

Includes: Everything in Tier 2, plus auth, dashboard, database, API integrations, role-based access, 3 revision rounds, 4 weeks post-launch support.

Excludes: Ongoing feature dev, mobile app, payment processing, complex backend.

Timeline: 6–12 weeks

Scoping Questions

  1. How many pages? (determines tier)
  2. Need auth/database? (Tier 2 vs 3)
  3. Do they have copy? (add copywriting or not)
  4. Do they have a brand? (add brand identity or not)
  5. Do they have images? (need to source or not)
  6. Timeline? (rush = premium)
  7. Need ongoing support? (add retainer)
  8. Budget? (qualify before investing time)

Quoting Rules

  1. Never quote before intake is filled out.
  2. 50% upfront, 50% on launch. No exceptions for new clients.
  3. Scope in writing. Not in scope = not included.
  4. Revisions are counted. 1-3 rounds.
  5. Rush fee = 25-50% premium.
  6. Say no to bad fits.
  7. Site audit as gateway. Paid audit first, rebuild second.

Red Flags ✕

  • "Can you do it for exposure?"
  • "My nephew could do this but he's busy"
  • "We need it tomorrow"
  • "We'll pay when we make money"
  • "Can you match this Fiverr quote?"
  • No clear business model
  • Can't say who their customer is
  • Want to approve every pixel

Green Flags ✓

  • Knows their customer well
  • Has existing revenue or funding
  • Respects your process
  • Has real testimonials to share
  • Websites are investments, not expenses
  • Responds promptly
  • Budget aligned with scope
