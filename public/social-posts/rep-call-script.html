Cold Call Script

Keep this beside you during every call. Know it, don't read it.

Step 1 — Opening (first 10 seconds)
"Hi, can I speak to the owner? … Hi [Name], my name is [Your Name], I'm calling from a web design studio called CrftdWeb. I'll be really quick — I'm not trying to sell you anything today, I just had a quick question."

Pause. Let them respond.
Step 2 — The Hook
"I came across your business online and noticed your website — honestly it looks like it could be doing a lot more for you. Is that something you've thought about at all?"

Wait for their response — don't fill the silence.
Step 3 — Their Response
✓ If Yes / "Yeah actually"
"Perfect. I work with a developer who specialises in building clean, fast websites for businesses like yours — usually starting from £500. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call with him to see if it's a fit?"
✗ If No / "We're fine"
"Totally get that. Out of curiosity, are you getting enquiries through the site regularly, or do most people find you another way?"

Gets them talking. If the site isn't converting, you're back in.
Step 4 — Book the Call
"Great — he's pretty flexible. I can get you a slot as early as [day]. What works better for you, mornings or afternoons?"

Always give a choice. Never ask "when are you free?" — too open ended.
Step 5 — If Not Interested
"No problem at all. Can I just send over a quick email with some examples of his work — just so you have it if things change?"

Gets the email. Turns a dead call into a warm lead for follow-up.

The Rules

  1. Never read it word for word — know it, then make it natural
  2. Your only goal is to book a call — not sell the website, that's his job
  3. Keep calls under 3 minutes — don't pitch, don't ramble
  4. Log every call — name, outcome, callback date in the Google Sheet
  5. Follow up everything — most closes happen on the 2nd or 3rd contact
