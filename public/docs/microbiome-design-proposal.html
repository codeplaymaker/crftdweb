Cold Email — Full Process

How the Cold Email Campaign Works

Cold email is the fastest route to a conversation. Done well, it's not spam — it's a targeted introduction to someone with a problem you can solve. Here's the full process.

1

Build the list

We identify three contact types: (A) University microbiome/environmental microbiology PIs in the UK whose recent publications show active sequencing work. (B) Biotech and agri-food companies in the UK with a known microbiome programme — sourced from Companies House new incorporations + LinkedIn. (C) Companies that have recently posted bioinformatician, microbiome scientist, or data analyst roles — sourced from LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed, and Otta weekly. Emails sourced via Hunter.io, Apollo, and lab "contact us" pages. Target: 200 contacts in the first batch, replenished monthly.

Week 3 — setup
2

The sequence — 3 emails, 10 days

Each contact gets a 3-touch sequence. No more. If there's no reply after 3 emails, they're removed. We never follow up beyond that.

Runs automatically via Instantly or Lemlist
3

Replies → you

Any positive reply gets forwarded to you immediately with context. You respond directly. We brief you on who they are and what they likely need before you reply. Goal: convert reply into a 20-minute scoping call.

The 3-Email Sequence
Email 1 — Day 1 · The hook

Subject: microbiome analysis — [University/Company name]

Hi [First name],

I came across your recent work on [specific paper topic or project] — interesting approach to [specific detail].

I'm a microbiome researcher at Swansea (published in Nature Comms and R Soc Open Sci) who consults on study design, sequencing strategy, and data interpretation for academic and commercial clients.

If you're at the point of needing analytical support — or just thinking about what the next study needs — I'm happy to have a quick call. No pitch, just a conversation.

Worth 20 minutes?

Matthew

Email 2 — Day 5 · The value add

Subject: Re: microbiome analysis — [University/Company name]

Hi [First name],

Following up briefly — I wrote something that might be useful regardless of whether we work together:

[Link to most relevant blog post — e.g. "Your Soil Test Is Lying to You" or study design mistakes post]

It covers [one-line description of what the post addresses]. Thought it might be relevant given your work on [topic].

Matthew

Email 3 — Day 10 · The close

Subject: Re: microbiome analysis — [University/Company name]

Hi [First name],

Last one from me — I know inboxes are noisy.

If you ever need support on microbiome study design, sequencing strategy, or analysis interpretation, I'm at [email]. I work with academic labs and commercial teams across the UK.

Best of luck with the research.

Matthew

Why this works: Email 1 is personal — it references their actual work. Email 2 gives value before asking for anything. Email 3 is a clean exit that leaves the door open. The sequence respects their time. The personalisation signals you're not a bot. The credentials (Nature Comms, Swansea) establish trust in one line.
For job board leads specifically: The message changes. Subject: "microbiome support while you hire". Body: acknowledge the role they're hiring for, position consultancy as a bridge — faster than hiring, lower cost, no headcount risk. These contacts are the highest-intent leads in the pipeline.
