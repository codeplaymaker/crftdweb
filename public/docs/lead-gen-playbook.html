Overview

What This Is

Cold email outreach at scale is one of the highest-margin services you can offer on top of web design. You build a targeted list of business owners, send short personalised emails daily, and book qualified calls for your clients.

This playbook covers everything: setting up the system, building lists, writing emails, and turning it into a recurring revenue stream.

The model in one sentence: Find the right people, send them a short message that sounds human, book a call, and charge a retainer to keep doing it every month.

The System

How It Works (5 Steps)

1

Define Your ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

Pick one niche to start — trades work best (plumbers, electricians, roofers). Same problems, same ads, easy to replicate. Target: owner-run businesses, 1–10 employees, outdated website or no Google reviews, UK cities.

2

Build a Targeted List

Use Google Maps scraping (Outscraper ~£20), visit their websites for emails, verify with Millionverifier (~£5/1,000). Target 100–200 contacts to start.

3

Set Up Sending Infrastructure

Buy a burner domain (e.g. crftdwebagency.co.uk ~£10/yr). Set up a Google Workspace mailbox (~£5/mo). Warm it up for 2 weeks using Instantly.ai or Mailreach before sending anything.

4

Write & Send Emails

Short, plain text, personal. 20–30 emails/day to start. Three follow-ups max (day 1, day 3, day 7). No logos, no HTML, no unsubscribe links in the first email.

5

Optimise Weekly

Track open rate (target >40%), reply rate (target >5%), positive reply rate (target >2%). Every reply and objection is data. Refine subject lines and openers weekly.

Email Writing

What to Send

Cold emails should look like they came from a real person — not a company. The moment it looks "branded" (logo, footer, colours) the brain reads it as marketing and ignores it.

✓ Plain text ✓ Short (under 80 words) ✓ One question at the end ✓ Personal sender name ✗ No logo ✗ No HTML template ✗ No "we are a company that..."
Example — Initial Email
Hi John,

Came across Riverside Plumbing — looks like the site hasn't been updated in a few years.

We helped a plumber in Manchester pick up 11 extra calls/mo after a rebuild.

Worth a quick chat?

James
Example — Follow-up (Day 3)
Hey John — just bumping this up in case it got buried.

Happy to show you what we did for the Manchester client if useful.

James
Example — Final Follow-up (Day 7)
Last one from me — if now's not a good time no worries at all.

If you ever want more calls coming through the site, you know where I am.

James

Client Setup

Setting Up a Campaign for a Client

Once you have your own system proven, you can run the same playbook for paying clients. Here's exactly how to onboard and set up a client campaign from scratch.

1

Client Intake — What You Need Before You Start

Before building anything, get clarity on who they are and who they want to reach. Ask:

  • What do you do and what's your best-selling service?
  • What kind of customer do you want more of? (area, size, budget)
  • What result do you get for your best customers? (e.g. "saved them £4k on their boiler")
  • Do you have any case studies, testimonials, or before/after results?
  • Who handles enquiries — you or someone else?
  • How fast do you respond to new leads? (critical for conversion)
  • What's your target area? (city / radius)
  • Any types of customer you don't want?
2

Build Their ICP

Use their answers to define exactly who you're targeting. Document it clearly:

  • Niche: e.g. residential plumbers, 1–5 staff
  • Location: e.g. Bristol, Bath, Swindon (30-mile radius)
  • Signals: outdated website, few Google reviews, no live chat
  • Exclusions: commercial only, national chains, already using a competitor
3

Set Up Their Sending Infrastructure

Never send from the client's main domain — it risks their reputation. Set up dedicated infrastructure for them:

  • Register a variation domain (e.g. getjohnplumbing.co.uk or johnplumbingbristol.co.uk)
  • Set up one Google Workspace mailbox (~£5/mo) — use a real first name
  • Configure SPF, DKIM, DMARC records (Instantly guides you through this)
  • Start inbox warmup — do not send cold emails for at least 14 days
4

Build Their List

Use their ICP to pull a targeted list of prospects for their business. These are people who could become their customers — not outreach targets for your agency.

  • Use Outscraper to pull businesses in their target area and vertical
  • Example: a solicitor client wants more SME clients → scrape "limited companies registered in Bristol last 12 months" via Companies House
  • Clean, verify, and load into Instantly under their campaign
5

Write Copy Using Their Voice & Results

The email should sound like it came from them (or their team), not from a generic agency. Use their actual results as proof.

Example — written for a plumber client
Hi [Name],

Noticed [Business] comes up for emergency plumbing in [City] — wanted to reach out directly.

We recently helped a homeowner in [area] cut their heating bill by 30% after a full system check. Happy to do a free 15-min call if you're ever looking for a reliable local plumber.

[Client first name]

Note: adjust the angle depending on whether you're emailing residential customers, estate agents, letting agents, etc.

6

Managed vs Handoff

Decide upfront which model you're running:

Model You Do Client Does Charge
Fully managed Everything — list, copy, sending, replies, reporting Show up to calls £750–1,500/mo
Done-with-you Setup + training Manage replies themselves £300–500/mo
Setup only Build the system once Run it themselves £500–1,000 one-off

Fully managed is the highest value and best for retention. Start here.

7

Monthly Reporting

Send a simple report every month. Clients don't care about technical metrics — they care about booked calls. Keep it short:

  • Emails sent this month
  • Open rate (target >40%)
  • Replies received (total + positive)
  • Calls booked this month
  • What we're testing next month (subject line, new angle, new area)
Keep it to one page. A client seeing "312 emails sent, 6 booked calls" is all they need. Over-reporting creates doubt.
8

Handling Replies

In the fully managed model, you manage replies on their behalf. Set expectations upfront:

  • Positive replies → you book the call directly into their calendar
  • Not now → tag for follow-up in 30–60 days
  • Unsubscribe requests → remove immediately, do not follow up
  • Negative replies → note the objection, use to refine targeting

Bonus Method

Companies House — New Business Prospecting

Companies House publishes every Ltd company incorporated in the UK — including the director's name, registered address, and date of incorporation. New businesses almost always need a website and are actively spending on setup. This is a free, high-quality, consistently refreshed lead source.

The insight: A company incorporated in the last 30–90 days has a director in "setup mode" — domain, bank account, accountant, insurance — and very likely no website yet. Your timing is perfect.
1

Pull New Incorporations

Go to find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk and search for companies incorporated in the last 30–90 days. Filter by postcode area (e.g. BS for Bristol). Or use the free Companies House API to pull this programmatically. No account required.

2

Filter Out Noise

Skip holding companies, investment vehicles, and property SPVs. Focus on SIC codes that indicate a real trading business: trades & construction (43xx), retail (47xx), professional services (69xx–74xx), hospitality (56xx). Sole traders registered as Ltd are a strong signal — it often means they just turned pro.

3

Find the Director's Email

Companies House gives you the director's name. Use that to find a contact email:

  • Search the company name on Google — some list an email on early social profiles
  • Check if they have a domain registered (WHOIS or just try the domain) — use Hunter.io to find emails on that domain
  • Search the director name + company on LinkedIn, then find their email via Apollo.io
  • If they're a trade (e.g. plumber/electrician), check Checkatrade, MyBuilder, or Yell for a contact email
4

Verify Before Sending

Run all found emails through Millionverifier before importing to Instantly. New business owners often use personal Gmail addresses — these are valid but won't show in Hunter. Include them.

5

Send a Targeted Email

Reference the company directly — you know it's new, so the timing angle is natural and non-creepy. Keep it short.

Example — Companies House cold email
Hi Sarah,

Noticed [Company Name] was recently incorporated — congrats on going Ltd.

Do you have a website sorted yet? We build clean, fast sites for Bristol businesses — usually live in 2 weeks, starting from £1,200.

Worth a quick chat?

Obi
